Thrust Factor

Thrust Factor is a tight, difficult, movement based space shooter, loosely based on the classic game Asteroids. The player avoids lasers, missiles and asteroids while battling bosses and gathering upgrade blips to improve their ship.

Design Goal

I wanted to modernize the retro game Asteroids. Apart from sound and graphics, I set out to improve four aspects:
  • Modernized controls
  • Faster and more active gameplay
  • Better enemy AI
  • More long-term goals

Controls

After multiple iterations, it turned out that in a top-down perspective, using the mouse to both aim and turn worked really well. It means you fire in the direction you're flying: the player will be pointed towards the danger.

Forcing Movement

Like in classic Asteroids, players initially remain stationary, turning the game into a turret sim. Instead of adding arbitrary mechanics, I decided to work with the tools I had, and design enemies that force the player to move.

Through playtesting I found that players got bored after a few runs: there was too little long-term progression. That's why I designed a meta loop: to sustain engagement, the player can buy permanent upgrades.
